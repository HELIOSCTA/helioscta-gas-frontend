Executive Summary

February 27, 2026 marks the cheapest AGT spot price of the month at $4.22/MMBtu, down from a staggering $30.24 peak on 2/6 during the early-February cold blast. The physical system loosened materially: Stony Point compressor throughput dropped 10.1% below the Feb MTD average to 1.637 BCF, driven by softening residential/commercial heating demand across New England. Total Net Take Away improved to -0.917 BCF (+0.096 vs prior day), confirming less gas was being pulled off the system.
Key dynamics on 2/27: The Iroquois interconnect at Brookfield dramatically reduced exports from AGT (-0.129 BCF vs -0.265 prior day), retaining 136 MDth of gas on the AGT system. Mendon receipts from TGP surged 35% to 0.243 BCF, providing downstream supply relief. CT Electric power generation demand bucked the trend, spiking to -0.256 BCF (+66 MDth), the highest in the trailing week.
Price signal: AGT basis collapsed to $1.29 (from $6.68 on 2/23), Iroquois Z2 basis fell to $0.65, and TETCO M3 traded at a -$0.34 discount to HH. All NE bases narrowed simultaneously, confirming regional demand softening rather than AGT-specific dynamics.

Compressor Station Throughput (BCF)

Compressor throughput tracks the gas pushed eastward through each station. Stony Point (blue) is the primary driver — its drop on 2/26 to 1.860 BCF sets the ceiling for all downstream supply. Note the tight correlation between Stony Point and all downstream stations. The early-Feb cold blast (2/7-2/8) shows peak throughput across the system, while 2/23 shows a secondary spike followed by rapid decline.

AGT Cash Price vs Stony Point Throughput

The physical-financial correlation is stark. When Stony Point throughput peaks during cold events, AGT cash spikes. The 2/6 price explosion to $30.24 occurred during maximum system utilization. The subsequent price collapse to $4.22 by 2/26 tracks the gradual throughput decline as demand eased. Note that prices are more volatile than throughput — the system runs near capacity even on mild days, but the marginal molecule's price is extremely sensitive to the supply-demand balance.

Northeast Basis Comparison (vs Henry Hub)

Basis measures the premium New England buyers pay over Gulf Coast (Henry Hub). The early-Feb cold blast drove AGT basis to $25.83 and Iroquois Z2 to $30.17 — extraordinary premiums reflecting pipeline capacity constraints. By 2/26, AGT basis compressed to $1.29 and Iroquois to $0.65. TETCO M3 actually traded at a discount (-$0.34) to HH, indicating surplus Appalachian supply pushing into the NE corridor.

Total System Receipts vs Deliveries (BCF)

Total receipts and deliveries track closely — AGT has no storage, so the system must balance daily. The gap between receipts and deliveries represents net linepack change. Note the early-Feb peak near 3.3 BCF (approaching the 3.09 Bcf/d design capacity), the mid-Feb dip, and the late-Feb decline toward 3.0 BCF as winter demand eases.
